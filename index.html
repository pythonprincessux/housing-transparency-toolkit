Helping renters see the real cost of housing before they apply.

The Housing Transparency Toolkit is a research-informed website designed to help renters identify hidden fees, compare listing transparency, and make more informed housing decisions before committing time, money, or personal information.

Explore the Toolkit View Research Findings

Why housing transparency matters

Finding an apartment should not require detective work. Many rental listings separate advertised rent from mandatory fees, utilities, deposits, parking costs, and qualification requirements. This can make it difficult for renters to understand the true monthly cost of housing before applying.

This project examines how hidden costs, fragmented disclosures, and unclear qualification criteria affect renter decision-making. The toolkit translates those findings into practical resources and design concepts that support clearer, more accessible housing information.

Research contributions

Housing Transparency Standard

A five-layer framework outlining the information rental listings should disclose before a renter applies.

Transparency Rubric

A scoring tool used to evaluate how clearly housing platforms disclose cost, eligibility, and lease information.

Failure Mode Catalog

A taxonomy of common transparency failures, including price decoupling, qualification gating, and fee fragmentation.

True-Cost Estimator

A prototype concept showing how rent, fees, utilities, and move-in costs can be combined into a clearer total.

Thesis artifact

This website serves as the public-facing design artifact for the Barrett Honors Thesis, Redesigning the Renter Journey: A Comparative UX Analysis of Housing Stability. It connects the study’s research findings to practical tools that renters, students, and early-career adults can use when evaluating housing options.
