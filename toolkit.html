Renter Transparency Toolkit

Use this toolkit before applying for an apartment to identify hidden fees, estimate true housing costs, compare listings more accurately, and ask better questions before paying an application fee.

How to use this toolkit

  1. Start with the transparency checklist to identify missing information.
  2. Use the monthly cost worksheet to estimate the real recurring cost.
  3. Use the move-in worksheet to estimate upfront costs.
  4. Ask the leasing office for anything the listing does not clearly disclose.
  5. Compare listings using true monthly cost, not advertised rent alone.

Transparency checklist

Before applying, confirm whether the listing clearly answers each question below.

Monthly Cost

  • What is the advertised monthly rent?
  • What required monthly fees are added?
  • Are utilities included, estimated, or billed separately?
  • Is parking required or optional?
  • Are pet fees or pet rent disclosed?

Move-In Costs

  • What is the application fee?
  • Is there an administrative fee?
  • What deposit is required?
  • Is first month’s rent due before move-in?
  • Are any other upfront charges required?

Qualification Requirements

  • What income is required?
  • Are credit score expectations disclosed?
  • Is a guarantor allowed?
  • Are background screening rules explained?
  • Are rental history requirements visible?

Lease Terms

  • What lease lengths are available?
  • Does the rent include a temporary concession?
  • What happens after the promotion ends?
  • Is renter’s insurance required?
  • Are renewal policies explained?

Questions to ask before applying

Cost Questions

  • Can you provide a full monthly cost breakdown in writing?
  • Are all required monthly fees included in the advertised price?
  • Are utilities billed by the property, the city, or a third party?
  • Are there any fees not shown on the listing page?

Application Questions

  • What income multiple is required?
  • What credit criteria are used?
  • Is the application fee refundable?
  • Can I review qualification requirements before applying?

Lease Questions

  • Does the listed rent include a temporary concession?
  • What will the rent become after the promotion ends?
  • What fees may increase during renewal?
  • Are there penalties for ending the lease early?

Documentation Questions

  • Can the fee schedule be sent before application?
  • Can qualification requirements be sent before application?
  • Can move-in charges be itemized?
  • Can lease terms be reviewed before paying deposits?

How to compare listings fairly

Do not compare apartments using base rent alone. Compare the estimated true monthly cost, total move-in cost, qualification requirements, lease terms, commute, accessibility, and the amount of missing information.

A listing with a lower advertised rent may become more expensive once required fees, utilities, parking, deposits, and temporary concessions are included.

Warning signs

  • The listing says “fees may apply” but does not list the amount.
  • Qualification requirements are only shown after starting an application.
  • Move-in costs are not itemized.
  • Rent concessions are advertised without showing the long-term monthly rent.
  • Utilities are described vaguely without an estimate or billing explanation.
  • The property cannot provide a written cost breakdown.
