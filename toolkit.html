Housing Transparency Toolkit

This toolkit translates the research findings into practical questions, checks, and resources renters can use before applying for housing. It is designed for students, early-career renters, and anyone comparing rental listings online.

Before you apply: transparency checklist

Use this checklist to identify missing information before paying an application fee or submitting personal information.

  • What is the advertised monthly rent?
  • What is the estimated true monthly cost after required fees?
  • Are utilities included, estimated, or billed separately?
  • Is parking required or optional?
  • Are there technology, trash, package, amenity, or pest-control fees?
  • What is the total move-in cost?
  • What income is required to qualify?
  • Are credit score expectations disclosed?
  • Is a guarantor allowed or required?
  • Are concessions temporary or reflected in the long-term rent?

Questions to ask the leasing office

  • Are all required monthly fees included in the advertised price?
  • Can you provide a full monthly cost breakdown in writing?
  • What fees are due before move-in?
  • Are utilities billed by the property, the city, or a third party?
  • What income, credit, or rental history requirements are used?
  • Does the listed rent include a temporary concession?
  • What will the rent become after the promotion ends?

How to compare listings fairly

Do not compare apartments using base rent alone. Compare the estimated true monthly cost, move-in cost, qualification requirements, lease terms, and the amount of missing information.

A listing with a lower advertised rent may be more expensive once required fees and utilities are included.
