About the Author

Hello! I'm Kiara McRae, a Human Systems Engineering (User Experience) student in Barrett, The Honors College at Arizona State University. This website was developed as the public-facing design artifact for my honors thesis exploring transparency in online rental housing.

Research Interests

My work focuses on applying human-centered design principles to complex decision-making environments. I am particularly interested in user experience research, information transparency, accessibility, decision support systems, artificial intelligence, and public-interest technology.

Through this project, I examined how housing platforms communicate financial and qualification information and how interface design can better support renters making high-stakes housing decisions.

About This Project

The Housing Transparency Toolkit was created to bridge academic research and practical application. Rather than simply identifying problems within online rental listings, the project proposes research-informed tools and design recommendations that can help renters better understand affordability before committing to a lease.

The toolkit includes the Housing Transparency Standard, Transparency Evaluation Rubric, Failure Mode Catalog, True-Cost Estimator, and renter-facing decision-support resources developed throughout the research process.

Future work will explore larger-scale evaluations of rental platforms, expanded usability testing, interactive transparency tools, and opportunities to improve housing affordability through human-centered technology and design.
