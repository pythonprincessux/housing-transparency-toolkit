Transparency Evaluation Rubric (TER)

The Transparency Evaluation Rubric (TER) was developed to evaluate how clearly online rental listings communicate information that renters need to make informed financial decisions. Rather than focusing on visual design alone, the rubric evaluates whether essential information is complete, accessible, and easy to understand.

Scoring System

Each criterion is scored using the following four-point scale.

Score Description
0 Information is missing or cannot be located.
1 Information is partially available but incomplete or difficult to find.
2 Information is mostly available with minor limitations.
3 Information is complete, clearly presented, and easy to locate.

Evaluation Criteria

Criterion What is Evaluated?
Financial Transparency Are recurring housing costs clearly disclosed beyond the advertised rent?
Fee Visibility Are required fees presented before the renter begins the application process?
Qualification Transparency Are income, credit, and guarantor requirements clearly communicated?
Information Completeness Can renters find the information they need without searching multiple pages?
Decision Support Does the listing help renters estimate affordability before applying?
Accessibility & Plain Language Is the information understandable, readable, and written in clear language?

Why the rubric matters

The Transparency Evaluation Rubric creates a repeatable method for comparing rental platforms. Instead of relying on personal opinion, listings can be evaluated using consistent criteria that reflect the information renters need most.

During this study, the rubric supported artifact analysis by documenting strengths and weaknesses across multiple rental listings and identifying recurring transparency failures.

Relationship to the research

The findings generated through the Transparency Evaluation Rubric informed the development of the Housing Transparency Standard, the Failure Mode Catalog, and the proposed True-Cost Estimator. Together, these design artifacts translate research findings into practical recommendations for improving housing transparency.
